How to Get Hired in Tech Without a Degree

You don’t need a computer science degree from a fancy university to land your first job in tech. What you do need is skill, proof of work, and strategy. Here’s how:

1. Pick a High-Demand Skill

Whether it’s front-end development, data analysis, UI/UX design, or AI—choose one area and dive deep. Free platforms like FreeCodeCamp, Coursera, or ALX can help you get started.

2. Build and Show Your Work

Create personal projects or contribute to open-source. Don’t just say you can code—show it with your GitHub, portfolio site, or LinkedIn posts.

3. Get Visible

Join tech communities on LinkedIn, Discord, or Twitter. Talk about what you’re building. Offer value. Visibility is leverage.

4. Tailor Your Resume

Make sure it’s results-driven. No fluff. Highlight your problem-solving skills and practical experience over formal education.

5. Network with Purpose

Reach out to people in the roles you want. Ask thoughtful questions. Show your progress. Ask for referrals. People hire who they trust, not who they don’t know.

If you’re consistent and confident, the door will open—even without a degree.
